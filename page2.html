1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects:

The GDPR exists to allow the consumer to have more control over their information which could affect the speed of any project that requires said information dependent on how compliant the consumer is and will force companies to be a lot more open with their use of information and restrict the misuse of said information. While that generalizes Article 15 it also ensure the each consumer has the 8 fundamental rights of the GDPR which are: 1: The right of access:
This allows for the “individuals the right to request a copy of any of their personal data which are being ‘processed’”.[1]

2: The right to be informed:
This give the consumer the ability to see how their data will be “collected, used, consulted or otherwise processed, and to what extent the personal data are, or will be, processed”[2] and will force the company holding the information to be “lawful, fair, and transparent”.[2]

3: The right to rectification:
This allows the consumer to change any information they believe to be inaccurate therefore allowing the consumers the “right to have the data rectified”.[3]

4: The right to erasure:
This can also be referred as the “right to be forgotten”[4] but this gives the consumer the ability and the “right to have your data erased”.[4]

5: The right to data portability:
This right helps both company and consumer with easing the flow of information while complying with the GDPR this gives the consumer the ability to “obtain your personal data from a data controller in a format that makes it easier to reuse your information”[5] under the right circumstances.

6: Your rights in relation to automated decision making, including profiling:
This will force a company to ask whether the consumer would or would not like to have to be a “subject to a decision based solely on automated processing”[6]

7: The right to object to processing of personal data:
This will allow the consumer the “right to object to certain types of processing of your personal data where this processing is carried out in connection with tasks:

  • in the public interest,
  • under official authority,
  • or in the legitimate interests of others".[7]

8: The right of restriction:
While this is limited the consumer does have a “right of restriction of processing of your personal data by a data controller”[8] and this will allow a company to store the consumer information but will prevent them from most processing actions for example deletion, unless they have your permission.
If a company were not to comply with GDPR they will be fined up to “17 million euros, or 4% of a company’s annual turnover.”[9] while that would be legal ramification their is also a social ramification of losing trust of your consumers.
